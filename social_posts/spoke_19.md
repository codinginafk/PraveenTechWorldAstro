Most "unsolvable" system crashes aren't CPU or SSD failures.

They are ghost errors hiding in your RAM.

Stop guessing and start isolating.

If your system is hitting BSODs or random freezes, your diagnostic workflow is likely broken. Here is how to isolate failing DIMMs without wasting hours of downtime:

1. **Isolate the Slot:** Don't test all sticks at once. Run a single-stick test in every motherboard slot to determine if you have a faulty module or a faulty motherboard trace.
2. **Stress Test the Logic:** Use `mdsched.exe` for quick checks, but use PassMark MemTest86 via USB for deep, OS-agnostic verification of every memory cell.
3. **Physical Maintenance:** Use 99% isopropyl alcohol to clean the gold contacts; static or oxidation is often the culprit, not the silicon itself.
