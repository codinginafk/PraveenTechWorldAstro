Sharing company passwords over Slack, spreadsheets, or sticky notes is an operational disaster waiting to happen.

Managing IT operations across 700+ users taught our team one hard lesson:

Offboarding an employee without automated credential revocation leaves massive security backdoors open into corporate infrastructure.

Consumer password managers don't solve this for businesses. You need:

→ SAML 2.0 / OpenID Connect SSO integration (so users log in with Entra ID or Okta)
→ SCIM Auto-Provisioning (so terminating an employee in HR instantly revokes vault access)
→ SIEM Audit Logging (for SOC 2 Type II & ISO 27001 compliance)

My team evaluated the top 4 business password managers for 2026: Bitwarden Enterprise, 1Password Business, Keeper, and Dashlane.

Here is what actually matters for IT teams:

1/ Bitwarden Enterprise ($6/user/mo) is the open-source king. You can self-host it on Docker or air-gapped Linux servers, keeping 100% custody of encrypted database files. Plus every employee gets a free family plan.

2/ 1Password Business ($7.99/user/mo) has the best developer CLI and SSH key integration for DevOps teams.

3/ Keeper Enterprise ($5/user/mo) is FedRAMP authorized for strict military/healthcare compliance.
