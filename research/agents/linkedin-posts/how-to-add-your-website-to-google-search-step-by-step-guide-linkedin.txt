Spent last week building a new site. Did the "site:yourdomain.com" search on Google and got nothing.

Not a single result.

It is a familiar feeling. You put in the work, hit publish, and then wait. Google does not know you exist. You have to show it the way.

I tested four methods to get indexed. Here is what I found:

Method 1: Google Search Console (non-negotiable)
This is the front door. Verify your property, submit your URL in the URL Inspection tool, and click Request Indexing. Google crawls it within minutes.

Method 2: Sitemap submission (most comprehensive)
A sitemap tells Google every page you want indexed. Most site builders generate one automatically. Submit yours in Search Console and let Google do the rest.

Method 3: IndexNow (speed hack)
Bing, Yandex, and Seznam support IndexNow. Google does not directly, but faster indexing on Bing typically means Google follows sooner.

Method 4: Stop trying to trick Google
Skip the "submit to 100 search engines" advice. Skip paid indexing services. Skip black-hat tricks. None of them work. Google finds you faster when you publish real content and get real backlinks.

My site hit indexed pages within 24 hours using this process. Still early days, but Google is already showing impressions for relevant searches.
