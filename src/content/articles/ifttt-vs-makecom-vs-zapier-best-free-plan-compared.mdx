## Why This Matters (a real-world problem this solves)

You know that feeling? You're copying and pasting data between apps all day. Maybe you're saving email attachments to Google Drive, then manually updating a spreadsheet. Or you're posting the same announcement across five social media platforms, one by one. It's a giant time suck.

Automation tools promise to fix this. They connect your apps and run tasks for you. But here's the catch: their powerful features are all on paid plans, and the free tiers are confusingly different. You don't want to waste an hour setting everything up, only to hit a paywall on your first real task.

I've been there. I tried to automate a simple blog workflow and ran into different free plan limits on each platform. It was frustrating. This guide breaks down exactly what you get with IFTTT, Make.com, and Zapier for free. That way, you can pick the right one and get back to actually doing your work.

## What You Will Learn

-   **My personal recommendation** for students vs. office workers.

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Get Started With Each Platform
First, sign up for the free accounts. It only takes 5 minutes.
1.  Go to **IFTTT.com**, **Make.com**, and **Zapier.com**.
2.  Create an account with your email or Google login.
3.  Look at their dashboard. You'll see a "Create" button. IFTTT calls them "Applets," Make calls them "Scenarios," and Zapier calls them "Zaps."

### Compare Their Free Plan Limits
This is the most important part. Let’s talk numbers.
-   **IFTTT:** You get **2 free Applets** that can run **unlimited times per month**. That's it. You can create more than two, but only two can be active at once.
-   **Zapier:** You get **100 tasks per month**. One task is one action. Saving one email attachment counts as one task, for example.
-   **Make.com (formerly Integromat):** You get **1,000 operations per month**. An operation is a step in your automation. This is more generous than Zapier, but complex flows use more operations.

### Connect Your First App: A Simple Example
Let’s automate saving email attachments to Google Drive. It’s a classic.
-   **On IFTTT:** Search for "Email attachments to Google Drive." There's a pre-built Applet. Turn it on. You've just used one of your two active slots.
-   **On Zapier:** Click "Create Zap." Set the trigger as "New Attachment in Gmail." Set the action as "Upload File in Google Drive." This would use 1 task.
-   **On Make.com:** Create a new Scenario. Add a Gmail module for "Watch Emails." Add a Google Drive module for "Upload a File." Connect them. This might use 2-3 operations.

### Pick the Winner for Your Specific Need
Your choice depends on what you're automating.
-   **Choose IFTTT if:** You need **two very specific, high-volume automations**. Think "Every time I post on Instagram, save it to Dropbox." Those two can run all month without counting against a limit.
-   **Choose Make.com if:** You need **more complex automations** with multiple steps and you want a higher monthly operation count. It's great for office workflows.
-   **Choose Zapier if:** You need **to automate many different, simple tasks** and you're okay with a lower total monthly task count.

## Tips for Best Results

1.  **Start with one task.** Don't try to automate your entire life at once. Pick the biggest time-waster, like social media cross-posting, and automate just that.
2.  **Combine platforms.** Use your 2 free IFTTT Applets for your heaviest tasks. Use your 1,000 free Make operations for your complex, multi-step work. This maximizes your total free automation capacity.
3.  **Use filters.** Both Make and Zapier let you filter data so your automations only run under specific conditions. This saves your precious free tasks and operations. Don't save *every* email attachment—only the ones from your boss.
4.  **Name your automations clearly.** Call them "Save Client PDFs," not "Scenario 1." You'll thank yourself later when you're managing multiple free connections.
5.  **Check the run history.** All three platforms show you a log of your automations. If something breaks, you'll see why. 90% of issues are just disconnected app permissions.
6.  **Know the limits are monthly.** Your task or operation count resets every month. If you hit your limit on the 25th, you just have to wait a few days.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What happens when I hit my free plan limit on Zapier or Make?**
Your Zaps or Scenarios will pause automatically. They won't delete, and they'll resume on the first of next month when your count resets. You won't lose your work.

**2. Can I connect apps like Notion, Slack, or Facebook?**
Yes. All three platforms support hundreds of popular apps on their free plans. The list is huge, and you can likely connect your favorite tools without upgrading.

**3. Is there a way to get more than 2 active automations on IFTTT for free?**
Not really. You can create many Applets, but you can only have two "on" at any time. If you need three or more always-on automations, IFTTT's free plan won't work for you.

## Conclusion

So, which free plan is best? It’s not about which platform is “better.” It’s about which limit structure matches your needs. For two heavy-hitter automations, IFTTT is perfect. For many small, varied tasks, Make.com’s 1,000 operations give you the most room. Zapier sits in the middle.

My advice? Start with the one that connects your most important app instantly. You can always switch later, since you're not paying anything.
