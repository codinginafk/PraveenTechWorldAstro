My AI article pipeline had 12 components. When I showed the blueprint to a technical SEO expert, their feedback hit hard: only 5 components actually worked well. Three were useless. One was actively sabotaging my content.

Here's what I changed and why it matters.

## The Original Pipeline

Over 70 articles, my system had grown messy with:

- A made-up **Information Density formula** (`(entities + facts) / word count`) that supposedly measured article quality  
- **Em dash policing** that flagged every dash as an AI giveaway  
- **Forced question headings** - every H2 had to be interrogative  
- **Automatic speakable schema** injected everywhere  
- A rigid **20-40 word answer** requirement after each heading  
- An **Obsidian RAG** limited to just my vault notes  
- An **Information Gain** tool extracting entities and facts  

I thought this was comprehensive. The expert showed me how wrong I was.

## What Got Cut

### The Information Density Formula (Rating: 5/10)

That `ID = (E + F) / W` formula? Complete fiction. Google never said they rank by entity density. The expert called it out as pure speculation - and they were absolutely right.

I replaced it with a **Rich Quality Score** that tracks what actually matters:

- Real-world examples  
- Actual commands and code snippets  
- Performance benchmarks  
- Experimental results  
- Documented failures  
- Honest tradeoffs  
- Visual aids like screenshots  

Now an LLM analyzes articles for these evidence types. Score 7+? High quality. Below 3? Needs work.

### Em Dash Cleanup (Rating: 1/10)

This made the expert chuckle. No search engine or AI model cares about em dash frequency. At all.

I deleted this metric from `content_auditor.py`. Wasted effort on a non-issue.

### Forced Question Headings (Removed)

Requiring every H2 as a question was unnatural. The expert recommended 30-50% questions - not 100%. "What Are Docker Layers?" sounds robotic compared to the natural "Docker Layers."

I modified `enforce_inverted_pyramid` in `apply_fixes.py` to flag only extreme cases (below 20% or above 70% questions).

### 20-40 Word Answers (Replaced)

Instead of rigid answer blocks, the new flow is: **heading → brief intro → detailed explanation**. Because that's how humans actually read.

### Speakable Schema (Deprioritized)

I'd automated schema markup for voice search. The expert confirmed: barely supported by Google, useless for tech content. Removed from `check_meta_faq.py`.

## What Improved

### Obsidian RAG → Full Knowledge Search (Rating: 10/10)

The old system only searched `research/vault/Published/`. The expert said: "Use everything you've got."

The new `obsidian_rag.py` searches seven knowledge bases:

- `research/vault/` - Core notes  
- `research/experiments/` - Test results  
- `research/terminal-logs/` - Command history  
- `research/benchmarks/` - Performance data  
- `research/bookmarks/` - Saved resources  
- `research/github-notes/` - PR discussions  
- `research/project-logs/` - Postmortems  

Now every article starts with relevant context from across my digital brain.

### Information Gain → Evidence Focus (Kept, refined)

The core idea held up: build on your own experiments and data. That's genuinely hard for AI farms to replicate.

I kept the tool but changed its scoring - now it tracks original evidence, not just keyword stuffing.

## The Results

After changes, the system runs clean:

- **315 pages** generated  
- **0 errors**  
- **~45 second builds**  
- **Pagefind indexed 315 pages (~8k words)**  

Article count grew from 69 to 70, but the real win is every new piece now has:

1. A **Rich Quality Score** based on evidence  
2. **Context** from my entire knowledge base  
3. A **Quick Summary** for humans and machines  
4. **Natural headings** - questions where they fit  

## Key Takeaways

The biggest lesson? **Stop optimizing for imaginary signals.**  

That density formula, em dash rule, forced questions, and voice markup? All chasing ghosts. They added complexity without benefit.

What actually worked was simpler:

- Use real experimental data  
- Let structure follow content  
- Cut useless complexity  

The expert nailed it: "Your edge isn't more content - it's more original evidence."  

That quote now lives above my monitor.
