My AI article pipeline had 12 components. I sent the blueprint to a technical SEO and AI visibility advisor for review. Their verdict: 5 components were excellent. 3 were useless. 1 was actively making things worse.

This is what I changed and why.

## The Pipeline Before

My orchestration system had grown organically over 70 articles. It included:

- An **Information Density formula** — `(entities + facts) / word count` — that scored articles on entity density
- **Em dash cleanup** that flagged every em/en dash as an AI writing pattern
- **Forced H2 questions** — every subheading had to be a question
- **Speakable schema** injection across all articles
- A **20-40 word answer block** requirement after each heading
- An **Obsidian RAG** that only searched the vault notes directory
- An **Information Gain** engine that extracted entities and facts

I thought I was being thorough. The advisor showed me where I was wrong.

## What Got Thrown Out

### The Information Density Formula (Score: 5/10)

The formula `ID = (E + F) / W` was completely invented. Google has never said they rank pages by entity density. The advisor called it "made up" — and they were right.

I replaced it with a **Rich Quality Score** that counts what actually matters:

- Original examples
- Real commands and code
- Benchmarks and performance data
- Statistics from actual experiments
- Mistakes and failures documented
- Tradeoffs discussed
- Screenshots and diagrams

The new score is computed by an LLM that reads the article body and counts each evidence type. An article scoring 7+ across these dimensions is "high quality." Below 3 is "low."

### Em Dash Cleanup (Score: 1/10)

This made the advisor laugh. Google does not care about em dashes. ChatGPT does not care. Claude does not care. Gemini does not care.

I removed the em dash density metric from `content_auditor.py` entirely. It was wasting engineering time on a non-issue.

### Forced H2 Questions (Removed)

The blueprint required every H2 to be a question. The advisor disagreed — aim for 30-50%, not 100%. They showed me why "What Are Docker Layers?" sounds robotic when the natural heading is "Docker Layers."

I rewrote the `enforce_inverted_pyramid` function in `apply_fixes.py` to flag only extreme ratios (below 20% or above 70%) instead of requiring every heading to be a question.

### The 20-40 Word Answer Block (Replaced)

Instead of forcing a short answer after every heading, the new structure is: **heading → one paragraph → then deep explanation**. Not because AI needs it. Because humans do.

### Speakable Schema (Deprioritized)

I had wired speakable schema generation into both the article template and the frontmatter injector. The advisor confirmed: speakable is "barely supported" by Google and "almost useless" for a tech blog. I removed the automated injection from `check_meta_faq.py`.

## What Got Expanded

### Obsidian RAG → Multi-Directory Knowledge Search (Score: 10/10)

The old RAG pipeline only searched `research/vault/Published/`. The advisor said to expand it to every knowledge source available.

The new `obsidian_rag.py` searches 7 directories:

- `research/vault/` — Obsidian notes
- `research/experiments/` — Experiment logs and results
- `research/terminal-logs/` — Command history and terminal output
- `research/benchmarks/` — Benchmark data
- `research/bookmarks/` — Saved resources
- `research/github-notes/` — PR and issue notes
- `research/project-logs/` — Postmortems and decisions

Each directory is searchable by query. The orchestrator calls this pipeline before generating every article, injecting relevant context into the LLM prompt.

### Information Gain → Evidence-Based Quality (Kept, refocused)

The advisor confirmed the core idea is excellent: use your own experiences, logs, and experiments as source material. That is genuinely hard for generic AI sites to copy.

I kept the concept but replaced the scoring methodology. Instead of counting entities and facts (which rewards keyword stuffing), the new system checks whether an article contains original evidence.

## The Measurable Result

After all changes, the build runs clean:

- **315 pages** generated
- **0 build errors**
- **44.83 second build time**
- **Pagefind indexed 315 pages with 7,995 words**

The number of articles went from 69 to 70. But more importantly, every new article now arrives with:

1. A **Rich Quality Score** that measures evidence density, not keyword density
2. **Retrieval-augmented context** from across the knowledge base
3. A **Quick Summary** for both human readers and retrieval systems
4. **Natural heading structure** — some questions, some statements — not forced either way

## What I Learned

The biggest lesson: **don't optimize for things Google hasn't asked for.**

The Information Density formula, em dash cleanup, forced H2 questions, and speakable schema were all trying to optimize for hypothetical ranking signals that don't exist. They added complexity without measurable benefit.

The things that actually moved the needle were simpler:

- Use real data from real experiments
- Let the article structure follow the content, not a template
- Remove complexity that doesn't serve a measurable goal

The advisor summed it up: "Your biggest opportunity isn't generating more content — it's generating more original evidence."

I'm keeping that sentence pinned to my workflow.
