---# Why your PC takes 5 minutes to boot (and how to fix it)

You know the drill. You press the power button, grab a coffee, and come back to a desktop still stuck loading. It’s more than just annoying—it eats into your productivity before the day even starts. The good news? You can often cut minutes off that boot time by turning off the junk that loads with Windows.

## Why this matters

A slow startup isn’t just a minor nuisance. Every minute you wait is a minute you could be working, studying, or just moving on with things. Over a week, that adds up to maybe 30 minutes of lost time. It’s not just the wait, either. That bloatware running in the background drags down your whole computer, not just the startup. Turning it off can make everything feel snappier, from opening files to switching between apps.

## What you’ll learn here

*   What bloatware is and how to spot it on your machine.
*   Two safe, built-in Windows methods to disable startup programs.
*   How to uninstall the apps you really don’t need at all.
*   What to do if you’re unsure about a specific program.
*   Quick tips to keep your startup fast for good.

## Step-by-step guide

### 1. Open Task Manager’s startup tab
This is your command center for startup programs. The fastest way to get there is to press **Ctrl + Shift + Esc**. If that doesn’t work, right-click the taskbar and select “Task Manager.” Once it’s open, click the “Startup” tab at the top.

### 2. Review and disable the culprits
You’ll see a list of programs, their publisher, and their “Startup impact.” Look for programs with a “High” or “Medium” impact. You’ll probably see things like Spotify, Microsoft OneDrive, Skype, or software from your printer manufacturer. Don’t just disable everything blindly.

Right-click on a program you never use at startup (like a chat app you only open when needed) and select “Disable.” The status will change. That’s it. The program stays on your computer, but it won’t launch automatically anymore.

### 3. Clean house with startup apps settings
Windows has another, simpler place to check. Press **Windows key + I** to open Settings. Go to “Apps,” then click “Startup” on the right-hand side. Here you’ll see a clean list with on/off toggles. It’s easy to flip off anything you don’t recognize or need immediately. I turned off about 10 things here on my old laptop, and it cut my boot time in half.

### 4. Uninstall what you truly don’t need
Disabling is great, but uninstalling is better. That “Game Bar” you’ve never used? Uninstall it. That third-party PDF viewer that came with the PC? Get rid of it. Head back to Settings > Apps > Installed apps. Sort the list by “Install date” to see the oldest apps first. Click the three dots next to any junk and choose “Uninstall.”

### 5. Be careful with antivirus and system tools
This is the golden rule. If you don’t know what it is, leave it alone. Never disable your antivirus software, anything from your PC manufacturer (like Dell or HP utilities that manage your battery or drivers), or anything that says “Microsoft Corporation” unless you’re 100% sure it’s something like Cortana or OneDrive you don’t use.

## Tips for best results

1.  **Make a note before you start.** If you disable something and your computer acts weird, you’ll know exactly what to re-enable.
2.  **Focus on high-impact programs first.** You’ll get the biggest speed boost by tackling the apps labeled “High” startup impact.
3.  **Don’t forget your web browser.** Sometimes browser extensions can set themselves to run at startup. Check your browser’s settings too.
4.  **An SSD is the ultimate upgrade.** Disabling bloatware helps, but moving from a hard drive to a Solid State Drive (SSD) is like going from a bicycle to a motorcycle. Boot times drop from minutes to seconds.
5.  **Run a regular maintenance check.** Every few months, pop back into Task Manager or Startup Apps settings. New software installs often re-enable themselves.
6.  **Consider a fresh Windows install.** If your system is years old and cluttered beyond repair, backing up your data and reinstalling Windows can give you a completely fresh, fast start.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: I disabled something and now a feature I use is broken. What do I do?**
A: Don’t panic. Open Task Manager or Startup Apps settings again. Find the program in the list, right-click it (or toggle it on), and select “Enable.” Restart your computer, and everything should be back to normal.

**Q: Can disabling startup programs speed up my computer while I’m using it, or just during boot?**
A: It helps both ways. Programs that load at startup often keep running in the background, using up your CPU and RAM even when you’re not using them. Disabling them frees up those resources for the tasks you actually care about.

**Q: Is it safe to use third-party “PC cleaner” apps to manage startup programs?**
A: I’d avoid them. Many are aggressive, come bundled with other junk, or even cause more problems. The built-in Windows tools (Task Manager and Settings) are completely safe, free, and all you really need.

## Conclusion

You don’t need a new computer for a faster startup. Just take 15 minutes to audit what’s loading with Windows. Disable the clutter, uninstall the useless apps, and you’ll be amazed at how much quicker your PC is ready to go. It’s one of the easiest fixes you can do today.
