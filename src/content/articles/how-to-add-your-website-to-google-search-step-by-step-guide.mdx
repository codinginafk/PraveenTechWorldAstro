You built your website. Looks great. But when you search `site:yourdomain.com` on Google, nothing shows up.

This is normal because Google isn’t aware of your site yet, you need to notify it. Fortunately, this process takes just 30 minutes and is completely free. If you’re still in the process of building your site, refer to our [complete beginner guide to building a website] first.(/blog/how-to-build-a-website-from-scratch-in-2026-a-complete-beginner-guide).

Here is the exact process I used to get praveentechworld.com indexed in under 24 hours.

## Method 1: Google Search Console (Required)

Every method on this list starts with Google Search Console. It is the only official way to tell Google your site exists.

If you have not set it up yet, read our [full GSC setup guide](/blog/how-to-set-up-google-search-console-for-your-new-website) first. It walks through verification, property types, and the initial configuration.

Once verified, submit your URL directly:

1. Open the URL Inspection tool in Search Console
2. Paste your homepage URL
3. Click "Request Indexing"

Google typically crawls your URL within minutes and adds it to the index. While effective for individual pages, this method isn’t the most efficient for indexing an entire site.

## Method 2: Submit Your Sitemap

A sitemap is a file that lists every page on your site Google should index. Most modern site builders generate one automatically.

Find your sitemap at `yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml`. If you see XML content with a list of URLs, you are good. If you get a 404 or an error, check our guide on [fixing sitemap errors](/blog/how-to-fix-sitemap-errors-in-google-search-console).

Submit it in Search Console:

1. Go to Sitemaps section in the left menu
2. Paste `sitemap.xml` in the field
3. Click Submit

Google periodically checks sitemaps and crawls all listed URLs. This is the most effective way to add your entire website to Google search at once.

## Method 3: Use IndexNow (Speed Boost)

IndexNow is a tool that prompts search engines to crawl your URLs right away. Bing, Yandex, and Seznam support it. Google does not directly support IndexNow but the faster Bing indexes you, the faster Google typically follows.

Most hosting platforms and CMS tools support IndexNow out of the box. If yours does, enable it in your settings. The process involves sending a quick notification to Bing’s IndexNow service with your URLs.

For optimal results, use IndexNow alongside sitemap submission. The two methods complement each other.

## Method 4: Let Google Find You Naturally

Search engines like Google continuously scan the web for new content. If other sites link to yours, Google will eventually find those links and discover your site.

Skip the old "Submit URL to Google" page. Google discontinued that feature in 2020. Anyone telling you to paste your URL into a form on google.com is giving outdated advice.

Instead, focus on:

- Publishing content that gets backlinks naturally
- Sharing your articles on social platforms like LinkedIn and X
- Syndicating your content to Dev.to or Medium (these sites have high authority and get crawled fast)
- Keeping your sitemap updated as you add new pages

## What Not to Do

Some "SEO experts" will tell you to submit your URL to hundreds of search engines. Do not do this. Google, Bing, and Yahoo cover 95%+ of all search traffic. Everything else is noise.

Avoid “guaranteed indexing” services, as they often turn out to be scams. Indexing depends on content quality, site structure, and server availability. No service can bypass these.

Do not use black-hat techniques like cloaking or doorway pages. Google penalizes sites that attempt to game search results. Recovering from penalties can take months or necessitate a formal reconsideration request.

## Troubleshooting: Google Found My Site But Nothing Shows Up

If your site is indexed but doesn’t appear in search results, the problem lies elsewhere. Check our guide on [Google Search Console showing no data](/blog/google-search-console-not-showing-data-8-fixes) for specific fixes.

Common reasons Google finds but ignores your site:

- Recent domain name with no trust signals
- Thin content (under 300 words per page)
- Duplicate content from another indexed site
- Robots.txt blocking Googlebot
- Noindex tag on your pages

Check these before assuming the indexing process failed.

## How Long Does It Take?

With sitemap submission and URL inspection, expect initial indexing within 24 to 72 hours. Full content discovery takes 1 to 4 weeks depending on your site's authority and how often you publish.

My site hit 25 indexed pages within the first month using the methods above. As of June 2026, Google is showing impressions for searches like "add website to google search" and "google analytics page tracking" even though the site is only 30 days old.

Success hinges on maintaining consistency. Submit your site once, publish content consistently, and allow Google’s crawlers time to index your pages. If your sitemap stays updated and your content stays fresh, indexing takes care of itself.

## What to Do If Nothing Works

Sometimes you follow every step and Google still ignores your site. Here is what that usually means:

**Your domain is too new.** Google is cautious with recently registered domains. A domain registered last week has no history, no trust signals, and no backlinks. Google waits to see if the site is legitimate before investing crawl resources.

There is no shortcut for this. Keep publishing content, get indexed on Bing and other search engines first (they are less strict), and let your domain age. Most new domains start seeing regular Google crawling within 4-8 weeks.

**Your content is too thin.** If every page on your site has 200 words of generic text, Google has no reason to index or rank them. Each page should offer something unique: original research, personal experience, or detailed instructions not available on other sites.

**Your site has technical problems.** If Googlebot cannot reach your server, or your pages return 5xx errors, Google gives up and moves on. Run a site-wide health check using a tool like Screaming Frog or Sitebulb to catch these issues.

**Your site is blocked by manual action.** Check Google Search Console for Manual Actions under Security & Manual Actions. If there is a manual action listed, Google has decided your site violates its spam policies. You will need to fix the issue and submit a reconsideration request.

For all of these cases, the best approach is to fix what you can (content quality, technical issues) and wait for the rest. Google's crawlers visit more frequently as your site proves itself reliable over time.

## Tracking Your Progress

After submitting everything, track your indexing progress weekly:

- Check the Index Coverage report in Search Console
- Search `site:yourdomain.com` and count indexed pages
- Monitor the Sitemaps report for errors or warnings
- Check the URL Inspection tool for individual pages

I logged these numbers every Monday for the first two months. Seeing the indexed page count grow week over week was motivating even when search traffic was zero. The indexing came first. The traffic came later.
