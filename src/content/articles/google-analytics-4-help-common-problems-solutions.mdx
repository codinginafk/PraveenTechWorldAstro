## GA4 Not Working? You Are Not Alone.

You installed Google Analytics 4, waited two days, and opened the dashboard expecting to see traffic data. Instead you see zeros. Or the numbers look wrong. Or events that should be tracking are missing entirely.

I have been through every GA4 problem you can imagine across dozens of sites. The good news is most issues follow the same patterns. Once you know what to look for, you can fix them in minutes instead of hours.

Here are the most common GA4 problems and exactly how to solve them.

## Problem 1: GA4 Shows Zero Data or No Traffic

This is the most common GA4 problem and the most frustrating. You installed the tracking code but the reports are empty.

Start with the Realtime report. Open it and visit your site in a new tab. If your name does not appear in Active Users within 30 seconds, your tracking code is not sending data.

Check your measurement ID first. Go to Admin, then Data Streams, and select your web stream. Copy the measurement ID. It starts with G- followed by ten characters. Compare it with the ID in your site code. A single wrong character breaks everything.

If the ID is correct, check for browser extensions. Open your site in an incognito window with no extensions. If data appears in incognito but not in your normal browser, ad blockers are the cause. uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger, and Ghostery all block GA4 by default.

If you use a consent banner, check your consent integration. Without Google Consent Mode configured, GA4 may be blocked entirely until the user accepts cookies. See our [GA4 troubleshooting guide](/blog/ga4-not-tracking-visitors-12-troubleshooting-steps) for a full walkthrough of every possible fix.

## Problem 2: Data Discrepancies Between GA4 and Other Tools

Your GA4 numbers do not match your server logs, your CRM, or your ad platform. This is normal to some extent but large gaps indicate a problem.

GA4 uses first-party cookies and client-side tracking. Server logs record every request, including bots and crawlers that GA4 filters out. Ad platforms use their own tracking pixels with different attribution models. Some difference is expected.

But if the gap is larger than 30 percent, check for duplicate tracking code. Search your site source for your measurement ID. If it appears more than twice, you have duplicate tags inflating or conflicting with each other.

Also check cross-domain tracking. If your site spans multiple domains, configure cross-domain tracking in GA4 settings. Without it, users moving from your main site to your checkout are counted as new users or lost entirely.

For a deeper breakdown of how GA4 data compares to other analytics, read our [Google Analytics for beginners guide](/blog/google-analytics-for-beginners-how-to-track-your-website-traffic).

## Problem 3: Missing Events or Conversions

You set up event tracking but the events never show up in reports. You check DebugView and see nothing.

The first step is confirming your events are actually configured in GA4. Go to Configure and then Events. If you are using Google Tag Manager, check that your GA4 Event tag is firing correctly. Use GTM Preview mode to verify.

For custom events, remember that GA4 requires event names to follow specific rules. Names must start with a letter, contain only letters, numbers, and underscores, and cannot have spaces. They are case-sensitive. A typo in the event name creates a completely different event that goes nowhere.

If you use enhanced measurement, verify it is enabled in your data stream settings. Enhanced Measurement automatically tracks page views, scrolls, outbound clicks, site search, video engagement, and file downloads. But each toggle needs to be turned on individually.

## Problem 4: Setup and Configuration Mistakes

Many GA4 problems trace back to how the property was created and configured. These setup errors are easy to make and hard to spot if you do not know where to look.

First, check your data retention settings. Go to Admin, then Data Settings, and then Data Retention. The default is two months for user-level data. If you need historical data for comparisons, set it to 14 months. This affects exploration reports and audience creation.

Second, verify your data filters. GA4 data filters can include or exclude traffic based on rules. A filter set to Exclude All by mistake will hide everything. Go to Admin, Data Settings, and Data Filters to check.

Third, confirm your account permissions. If someone else set up the property, you might have restricted access. You need at least Viewer or Analyst role to see standard reports.

## Problem 5: GA4 Not Processing Data in Standard Reports

Your Realtime report shows visitors. DebugView confirms events are flowing. But your standard reports like Pages and Screens or Acquisition show no data.

This is a data processing delay issue. GA4 takes 24 to 48 hours to process data into standard reports. Google needs this time to deduplicate, model, and aggregate the data.

There is nothing wrong with your setup. Check the Realtime report for current activity and DebugView for event-level verification. If both are working, your data will appear in standard reports within two days.

The time of day also matters. GA4 processes data throughout the day but report updates are not instant. What you see in the morning may not include the last few hours of the previous day.

## Problem 6: Attribution and Channel Data Looks Wrong

GA4 uses data-driven attribution by default. This differs from the last-click attribution model that Universal Analytics used. Your channel breakdown will look different.

If your organic search traffic seems low, check whether GA4 is correctly categorizing your traffic. GA4 uses machine learning to classify channels and it sometimes misattributes sources. You can adjust channel rules in the GA4 admin panel under Channel Grouping.

Also check your UTM parameters. Consistent, properly formatted UTM tags are essential for accurate source reporting. Missing or inconsistent UTMs cause GA4 to lump traffic under Direct or Unassigned.

## FAQ

**Q: Why does GA4 show different numbers than Universal Analytics?**
**A:** GA4 uses an event-based data model while Universal Analytics used a session-based model. They count things differently. A single page view in UA is one session step. In GA4, every interaction is its own event. Privacy changes also affect GA4 more because it relies on first-party data and consent. Expect GA4 numbers to be 15 to 30 percent lower than UA for most sites.

**Q: How long does it take for GA4 to show data?**
**A:** The Realtime report and DebugView show data instantly. Standard reports take 24 to 48 hours to process. If nothing appears in Realtime after 30 minutes of installation, your tracking code has a problem. Check your measurement ID and installation method first.

**Q: Can I use GA4 without a cookie consent banner?**
**A:** Technically yes, but you should not. GA4 sends user data to Google servers. If you operate in the EU, UK, or California, privacy laws require you to get consent before tracking. Use Google Consent Mode with a consent management platform to handle this properly.

**Q: What is the (not set) label in GA4 reports?**
**A:** The (not set) label means GA4 received an event without a value for a specific dimension. Common causes include missing UTM parameters in your campaign URLs, unconfigured event parameters, or data processing delays. Review your tagging setup to identify which events are missing values.

**Q: Does GA4 work with Single Page Applications?**
**A:** Yes, but you need enhanced measurement or custom code. GA4's enhanced measurement automatically tracks page views based on browser history changes. If your SPA uses hash-based routing, you may need to manually send page_view events using gtag or Google Tag Manager.
